# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

Two conversations conducted during the week. Names kept to initials at their request.

---

## Interview 1 — R.K., college friend, works as a software developer at a mid-size company

**How we connected:** Old college friend. Asked him over WhatsApp if I could show him something I was building.
**Duration:** About 10 minutes.

I just asked him to look at the tool and tell me what he thought. He ran it with the tools he actually uses — GitHub Copilot (his company pays for it) and ChatGPT Plus (he pays personally).

He didn't say much at first, just clicked through the form. When the results came up showing he might be overpaying by keeping both, he said "yeah I've been meaning to cancel ChatGPT but I keep forgetting."

**Direct quotes:**
- "I don't really know what plan my company has me on, I just use whatever they set up."
- "The savings number is the thing that makes you actually do it. I knew I was wasting money but seeing the exact number is different."
- "Why does it ask for team size? I'm just one person."

**Most surprising thing:** He didn't know GitHub Copilot and Cursor were competing products. He thought Cursor was something different entirely. A lot of people probably don't know their tools overlap.

## Interview 2 — S., a friend who runs a small freelance design + development business

**How we connected:** Friend from school. She does freelance work and pays for a few AI tools herself.
**Duration:** About 15 minutes in person.

She uses Claude Pro and ChatGPT Plus both. When I asked why both, she said "Claude is better for writing, ChatGPT is what I know." She had never really thought about whether she needed both.

I showed her the tool on my laptop. She filled in her tools herself. It came back suggesting she could drop ChatGPT Plus since her use case (writing and design briefs) is covered by Claude Pro at the same price.

Her reaction was basically: "That's obvious now that you say it. Why am I paying $40 when I only need $20."

**Direct quotes:**
- "I just never sat down and looked at all of them together."
- "The form is simple enough. I was worried it would ask me technical stuff I don't know."
- "Can I save this and come back to it? Like if I add a new tool later?"

**Most surprising thing:** She asked if the shareable link would show her email to whoever she sends it to. She wanted to share it with her accountant but didn't want personal info visible. This directly led to making sure the public audit URL strips all identifying details.
