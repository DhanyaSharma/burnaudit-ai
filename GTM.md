# GTM.md

## Who exactly is the target user

Not "startups." The specific person is an **engineering manager or CTO at a 5-20 person startup** who approved adding AI tools for their team over the past year and has never gone back to check if they're still needed or on the right plan. They're not trying to cut costs as a mission — they just don't want to look careless when someone asks why the SaaS bill keeps going up.

Second target: **solo founders and indie hackers** paying for 2-3 AI tools simultaneously and genuinely unsure if they're overlapping.

---

## What they Google before wanting this

- "cursor vs github copilot which one"
- "is claude team plan worth it for small team"
- "chatgpt plus vs team plan startup"
- "how much does cursor cost for a team"
- "ai tools i actually need vs nice to have"

These people already know they're spending money. They're just not sure it's the right amount.

---

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0

**Week 1 — Post a personal story on X**
Not "I built a tool." Something like: "Audited my team's AI tools today. We were paying for Cursor AND GitHub Copilot for 8 people. $152/mo straight in the bin. Built a free tool to find this automatically." Real numbers, real story, tool link at the end. This format spreads because it's useful, not promotional.

**Week 2 — Submit to Hacker News as Show HN**
"Show HN: Free tool to find waste in your AI tool subscriptions." HN audience is exactly the target user. A working tool with a clear use case gets 100-300 visitors in a day if it hits the front page. Even a modest showing gets 30-50 visitors.

**Week 3 — Reddit posts**
Post in r/SideProject and r/ExperiencedDevs with a personal angle: "Found $300/mo in waste on my team's AI tools — built something to automate the check." Reddit responds well to honest personal stories. Don't pitch the tool directly.

**Week 4 — Direct DMs on X**
Message 20 founders who have recently posted about AI tools or dev costs. Not a template blast — a specific message referencing their post. "Saw you mentioned Cursor — built a free tool that checks if you're paying for overlapping tools, thought it might be useful."
