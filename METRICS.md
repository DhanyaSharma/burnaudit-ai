# METRICS.md

## North Star Metric

**Number of email leads captured per week from audits showing $100+ in savings.**

This is the right metric because it measures both product quality (the audit found real savings) and business value (Credex gets a lead). Total audits completed would just measure traffic. Revenue is too slow to track weekly at this stage.

---

## 3 Input Metrics That Drive It

**1. Audit completion rate**
How many people who land on the page actually finish the audit and click Generate.
Target: above 35%. If it drops, the form is too confusing or too long.

**2. High-savings rate**
How many completed audits find $100+ in savings.
Target: above 40%. If it's low, the engine rules aren't catching real waste or users are already on good plans.

**3. Email capture rate**
How many people who see $100+ in savings actually submit their email.
Target: above 25%. If it's low, the lead capture modal copy isn't working or it's appearing at the wrong time.

---

## What to Measure First

1. Did the audit complete — and what were the total savings
2. Did the user submit their email
3. Did anyone open the shareable URL (meaning audits are being shared)
4. Did anyone click "Book Credex Consultation"

These four events tell you if the product is working end to end.

---

## What Triggers a Pivot

If after 200 audits:
- Fewer than 10% of users capture their email → change the lead capture timing or copy
- Fewer than 20% of audits find any savings → the engine needs more rules or better pricing data
- Zero Credex consultation clicks → lower the $500/mo threshold or change the CTA copy
