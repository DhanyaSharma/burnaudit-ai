# ECONOMICS.md

## What a converted lead is worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits. A typical startup buying credits would spend around $2,000-3,000 upfront for a 6-month supply of tools they're already using.

Assuming 30% gross margin on credits: **each converted customer = ~$600-900 gross profit.**

I'll use $600 as the conservative number throughout.

---

## How someone goes from audit to customer

```
100 people complete an audit
→ 30 submit their email (30%)
→ 5 book a Credex consultation (15% of emails)
→ 2 buy credits (40% of consultations)

Result: 2 customers per 100 audits
```

At $600 gross profit each = **$1,200 gross profit per 100 audits.**

## What makes this profitable for Credex

The interesting number is not the website traffic. It's using the tool as a sales asset in Credex's existing outreach.

If the Credex sales team sends this tool to 500 warm prospects per month and 20% run the audit (100 audits), but at a 10% conversion rate instead of 2% because they're already warm — that's **10 customers/month × $600 = $6,000 gross profit/month** from one email sequence.

---

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 months

$1M ARR = roughly $83k/month in revenue.
At $2,500 average deal = 33 new customers needed per month.

For that to happen:
- Audit volume needs to reach 1,500+/month by month 9
- Credex needs to actively use the tool in outreach, not just let it sit as a passive web page
- Some customers need to reorder — credits are consumable, so 40% reorder rate means each customer is worth $2,500 × 1.4 = $3,500 lifetime

If those three things are true, $1M ARR by month 18 is realistic. If the tool stays passive, it probably lands around $100-200k ARR from organic traffic alone.
